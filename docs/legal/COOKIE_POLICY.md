## 2. What Are Cookies?

### 2.1 Definition

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website or use an application. Cookies contain information that is transferred to your device's hard drive and allow the website or app to recognize your device and remember certain information about your preferences or past actions.

### 2.2 How Cookies Work

When you visit the Adify Platform:

1. A cookie file is sent to your device
2. Your browser or app stores the cookie
3. On subsequent visits, the cookie is sent back to our servers
4. We read the cookie information to provide a personalized experience

### 2.3 Cookie Lifespan

**Session Cookies:**
- Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser or app
- Used to maintain your login session
- Deleted automatically when you exit

**Persistent Cookies:**
- Remain on your device for a set period (days, months, or years)
- Used to remember your preferences and settings
- Stored until expiration or manual deletion

## 3. Types of Cookies We Use

### 3.1 Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

These cookies are required for the Platform to function and cannot be disabled without severely impacting your experience.

**Purpose:**
- User authentication and login sessions
- Security and fraud prevention
- Load balancing and performance
- Remembering items in your account
- CSRF protection

**Examples:**
- `session_token` - Maintains your logged-in session
- `auth_token` - Verifies your authentication status
- `csrf_token` - Protects against cross-site request forgery attacks
- `load_balancer` - Distributes traffic across servers

**Lifespan:** Typically session-based or 1-7 days

**Legal Basis:** These cookies are necessary for contract performance (providing the service you requested) and do not require consent under GDPR/POPIA.

### 3.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization but are not essential for basic Platform operation.

**Purpose:**
- Remembering your preferences (language, region, display settings)
- Customizing your user interface
- Remembering whether you've dismissed notices or tooltips
- Storing your choice regarding cookie consent

**Examples:**
- `language_preference` - Remembers your language selection
- `theme_preference` - Stores your UI theme choice (light/dark mode)
- `cookie_consent` - Records your cookie preferences
- `notification_settings` - Remembers your notification preferences

**Lifespan:** 6 months to 2 years

**Legal Basis:** Legitimate interest in providing a personalized experience. You can disable these cookies through settings, though this may reduce functionality.

### 3.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with the Platform so we can improve performance and user experience.

**Purpose:**
- Understanding user behavior and usage patterns
- Identifying popular features and content
- Tracking page views and navigation paths
- Measuring performance and load times
- Detecting errors and technical issues
- A/B testing new features

**Examples:**
- `_ga` - Google Analytics: Distinguishes users
- `_gid` - Google Analytics: Distinguishes users (24-hour lifespan)
- `_gat` - Google Analytics: Throttles request rate
- `analytics_session` - Internal analytics session tracking
- `feature_test` - A/B testing variant assignment

**Third-Party Analytics Providers:**

  - Opt-out: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

**Lifespan:** 1 day to 2 years depending on cookie type

**Legal Basis:** Legitimate interest in improving the Platform. In some jurisdictions (e.g., EU), we request consent before setting analytics cookies.

## 4. Similar Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use other tracking technologies:

**Use Cases:**

### 4.2 Local Storage

HTML5 local storage allows us to store data locally on your device with no expiration date.

**Use Cases:**
- Caching data for offline functionality
- Storing large amounts of preference data
- Improving app performance

**Clearing:** Can be cleared through browser settings (similar to cookies).

### 4.3 Device Fingerprinting

Collecting device characteristics to create a unique identifier.

**Data Collected:**
- Device type and model
- Operating system and version
- Screen resolution
- Installed fonts
- Browser plugins
- Time zone

**Purpose:**
- Fraud detection and prevention

- Detecting multi-accounting violations

**Note:** Device fingerprinting is more persistent than cookies and cannot be easily deleted. We use it primarily for security purposes.

### 4.4 Mobile Advertising IDs

**Google Advertising ID (GAID) - Android:**
- Unique identifier for advertising purposes

- Resettable through device settings (Settings → Google → Ads → Reset advertising ID)

- Apple's advertising identifier
- Used for ad tracking (with user consent on iOS 14.5+)
- Resettable through device settings (Settings → Privacy → Advertising → Reset Advertising Identifier)

**Purpose:**
- Personalized advertising
- Ad performance measurement
- Attribution tracking

**Important:** These IDs are managed by Google/Apple, not by us directly.

## 7. Managing Cookie Preferences

You have several options to control cookies:

### 7.1 Platform Cookie Settings

**In-App Cookie Preferences:**
1. Go to Settings → Privacy → Cookie Preferences
2. Toggle cookie categories on/off:
   - Essential Cookies (cannot be disabled)
   - Functional Cookies (optional)
   - Analytics Cookies (optional)
   - Advertising Cookies (optional)
3. Save your preferences

**Cookie Consent Banner:**
- On first use, you'll see a cookie consent banner
- Choose "Accept All" or "Customize" to manage preferences
- Your choice is remembered via a cookie (ironic, but necessary!)

**Note:** Changes take effect immediately but may require app restart or page refresh.

### 7.2 Browser Cookie Settings

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through settings:

**Google Chrome:**
1. Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
2. Choose:
   - Allow all cookies
   - Block third-party cookies
   - Block all cookies

**Safari:**
1. Preferences → Privacy
2. Enable "Prevent cross-site tracking"
3. Manage "Block all cookies"

**Firefox:**
1. Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
2. Choose standard, strict, or custom protection

**Microsoft Edge:**
1. Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data
2. Manage cookie settings

**Important:** Blocking all cookies will prevent you from logging in and using the Platform.

### 7.3 Mobile Device Settings

**Android:**
- Settings → Google → Ads
  - Opt out of personalized ads
  - Reset advertising ID
- Chrome browser → Settings → Site settings → Cookies

**iOS:**
- Settings → Privacy → Tracking → Ask App Not to Track (iOS 14.5+)
- Settings → Privacy → Advertising
  - Limit Ad Tracking (older iOS)
  - Reset Advertising Identifier
- Safari → Settings → Privacy & Security → Prevent Cross-Site Tracking

### 7.4 Google Ad Settings

Control personalized advertising from Google:

1. Visit: https://www.google.com/settings/ads
2. Sign in to your Google account
3. Toggle "Ads Personalization" on/off
4. Manage ad topics and interests
5. View ad partners and opt out

**Note:** Opting out shows fewer personalized ads but doesn't reduce total ads shown.

### 7.5 Third-Party Opt-Out Tools

**Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA):**
- Website: http://optout.aboutads.info/

**Network Advertising Initiative (NAI):**
- Website: http://optout.networkadvertising.org/

**European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance (EDAA):**
- Website: http://www.youronlinechoices.eu/

Some browsers offer "Do Not Track" signals. Currently, there is no industry standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. The Adify Platform does not currently respond to DNT signals, but we respect other opt-out mechanisms described above.

## 10. Cookies and Security

### 10.1 Cookie Security Measures

We implement security measures for cookies:

**Secure Flag:**
- Cookies marked as "Secure" are only transmitted over HTTPS
- Prevents interception over unencrypted connections

**HttpOnly Flag:**
- Cookies marked as "HttpOnly" cannot be accessed by JavaScript
- Protects against cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks

**SameSite Attribute:**
- Restricts cookie sending in cross-site requests
- Protects against cross-site request forgery (CSRF) attacks

**Encryption:**
- Sensitive cookie data is encrypted
- Session tokens use strong cryptographic standards

### 10.2 Cookie-Related Security Risks

**Session Hijacking:**
- Risk: Attacker steals session cookie to impersonate you
- Mitigation: We use secure, HttpOnly cookies with short lifespans

**Cross-Site Scripting (XSS):**
- Risk: Malicious scripts steal cookies
- Mitigation: HttpOnly flag, input sanitization, Content Security Policy

**Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF):**
- Risk: Attacker tricks browser into sending authenticated requests
- Mitigation: CSRF tokens, SameSite cookies

**Cookie Injection:**
- Risk: Attacker injects malicious cookies
- Mitigation: Input validation, secure cookie attributes

### 10.3 Protecting Your Cookies

To protect yourself:

- Use strong, unique passwords
- Log out after using shared devices
- Keep your browser and app updated
- Be cautious on public Wi-Fi
- Don't click suspicious links that might execute cookie theft scripts
- Enable multi-factor authentication (if available)

### 10.4 Suspicious Cookie Activity

If you notice:

- Unexpected logins or activity
- Sessions that don't end when you log out
- Cookies appearing from unknown sources

**Action:** Contact support@adrevtechnologies.co.za immediately and change your password.
